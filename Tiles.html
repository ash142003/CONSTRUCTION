Which Tile Should You Use?

Ceramic Tiles

Affordable and versatile, ceramic tiles are best for walls, bathrooms, and kitchens. They come in endless colors/patterns but are less durable than porcelain for floors.

Porcelain Tiles

Highly durable and water-resistant, porcelain is ideal for both indoor and outdoor floors. Its density makes it suitable for heavy foot traffic and wet areas.

Vitrified Tiles

A budget-friendly alternative to natural stone, vitrified tiles offer a polished look with minimal maintenance. Perfect for living rooms and offices.

Natural Stone (Marble/Granite)

For a premium, timeless appeal, natural stone adds elegance to floors and countertops. Requires periodic sealing but offers unmatched beauty.

Mosaic Tiles

Small, decorative tiles perfect for creating intricate designs on walls, backsplashes, or pools. Available in glass, ceramic, or stone.
